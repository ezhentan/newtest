## Last edited on 2020-09-26

This post serves as a documentation of 14 Data Science projects I am committing to complete. Why am I doing this? Recently, I came across a video by Thomas Frank (from College Info Geek) about the 1% Rule, and it really got me thinking. In essence, the 1% rule states that with every new project or work, there should be an improvement of 1%. This may seem small, but with time and with more projects, it will not take long for the progress to increase exponentially.

As such, I decided to embark on a series of projects that I can do to kickstart the consistency that I would need to truly capitalize on the 1% rule. For this post, I have followed the project ideas from Terence Sâ€™s (2020) post, 14 Data Science Projects to do During Your 14 Day Quarantine, linked here. The projects consist of 3 Visualization, 6 Exploratory Data Analysis, 5 Predictive Modelling projects.

With this post, I aim to upload my outputs here, and list my learning points and/or improvements after each project. Additionally, all outputs will be from code, unless otherwise stated (when I have produced the outputs in Tableau).
